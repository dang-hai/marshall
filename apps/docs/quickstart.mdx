# Three steps. That's it.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download">
    Grab the [latest release](https://github.com/dang-hai/marshall/releases) for your Mac.

    *Apple Silicon or Intel — we've got both.*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Install">
    Open the `.dmg`, drag to Applications, launch.

    Grant mic access when prompted.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Join a call">
    Start your Zoom, Meet, or whatever.

    Marshall wakes up automatically. You'll see insights appear as you talk.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Capture your thoughts

Marshall gives you two ways to capture what matters during calls.

### Notes editor

Click **+ Quick Note** to open a blank page. Type what you need — context, decisions, follow-ups.

The editor supports lightweight formatting:
- `# Heading` or `## Subheading` for structure
- `- item` for bullet lists
- `> quote` for callouts
- `**bold**` and `*italic*` for emphasis
- `---` for dividers

Notes save automatically and stay on your home screen.

### Voice transcription

Look for the **soundwave icon** at the bottom of the screen. Tap it to start recording.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap the icon">
    The recorder expands. Marshall starts listening immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Speak">
    Your words appear in real-time as you talk.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stop">
    Hit the stop button. Marshall finalizes the transcript.
  </Step>
</Steps>

First-time users will be prompted to download a Whisper model. This runs locally on your Mac — nothing leaves your device.

<Tip>
**Pro move**: Keep Marshall open before your call starts. It catches everything from the first hello.
</Tip>
